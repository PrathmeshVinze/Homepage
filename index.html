About Me

I am Prathmesh Vinze, a researcher in fluid mechanics and soft matter physics. I recently completed my PhD at LadHyX, École Polytechnique, under the supervision of Prof. Sébastien Michelin. My doctoral work focused on the collective dynamics of self-propelling phoretic microswimmers under external forcings. Broadly, I am interested in using theoretical and semi-analytical methods to uncover the fundamental physics underlying complex phenomena in low-Reynolds-number flows.

Research Interests

My research focuses on mathematical modeling of solute, momentum, and heat transport. I study the dynamics of self-propelled microswimmers (Janus particles) under mechanical and chemical forcings, collective dynamics of phoretic suspensions in shear flows, and the use of kinetic and spectral methods to analyze these systems. I am expanding my research into complex fluids, non-Newtonian effects, and applications in active matter engineering, microfluidics, and biomedical systems.
