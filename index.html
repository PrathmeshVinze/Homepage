Research Interests

My research focuses on mathematical modeling of transport processes, especially in low Reynolds number flows. I investigate the dynamics of self-propelling microswimmers (Janus particles), including their collective behavior under mechanical and chemical forcings. This work advances microfluidic control, active matter engineering, and bio-inspired transport systems. I am now expanding my research into complex fluids, exploring viscoelastic and non-Newtonian effects in microswimmers, with implications for smart drug delivery and biomedical engineering.
